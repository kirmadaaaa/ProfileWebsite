Chess is a complex game with numerous tactics that can help you gain an advantage over your opponent.

Here are eight of the best tactics to improve your play:

Fork

A move that attacks two or more of your opponent's pieces simultaneously. Knights are particularly good at creating forks.

Know more
Pin

A move that restricts an opponent's piece from moving because doing so would expose a more valuable piece behind it. For example, pinning a knight to the king with a bishop.

Know more
Skewer:

The reverse of a pin. A move where a high-value piece (like the king or queen) is in front of a less valuable piece, and moving the high-value piece exposes the lower-value piece to capture.

Know more
Discovered Attack:

When one piece moves away, revealing an attack by another piece. If the revealed attack targets a valuable piece like the king or queen, it can be very powerful.

Know more
Double Check:

A check delivered by two pieces simultaneously. This is especially powerful because the only way to get out of a double check is to move the king.

Know more
Decoy:

A tactic where you lure an opponent's piece to a particular square to exploit it in some way. For example, sacrificing a piece to pull the opponent's king into a vulnerable position.
